# CyberTrail
In today's digital world, almost everyone relies on electronic devices such as smartphones and computers. As more aspects of life move online — including communication, education, banking, shopping, and entertainment — the importance of cybersecurity continues to grow. However, many people do not understand how to protect themselves from cyber threats because they believe cybersecurity is too technical to learn or because many educational resources are expensive or difficult for beginners to understand. In reality, many successful cyber attacks target people rather than technology, using techniques such as phishing, social engineering, and credential theft to exploit individuals with limited cybersecurity awareness. Improving users' cybersecurity awareness and online habits is therefore one of the most effective ways to reduce cyber risk, as safer individuals contribute to a safer wider digital community.

This project aims to make cybersecurity education engaging, accessible, and practical through interactive simulations based on realistic scenarios. Instead of simply reading about online safety, users make decisions throughout each scenario and experience the consequences of those choices in a safe environment. Each simulation features branching storylines, timed events, and a digital forensics investigation where users analyse evidence to determine how an attack occurred before learning the cybersecurity principles behind it. The project also includes a typewriter-style storytelling interface, interactive decision points, and replayable scenarios that encourage users to explore different outcomes and understand how small decisions can have significant consequences. By focusing on practical situations rather than technical jargon, with scenarios designed for different age groups, the project helps users develop safer online habits and become more confident in protecting themselves online.

The overall goal of the project is to improve cybersecurity awareness by helping users build practical, long-lasting habits that they can apply in their everyday digital lives.
